SMART PILL DISPENSER - WIRING & PINOUT GUIDE
============================================

This document details the exact hardware connections required to assemble the Smart Pill Dispenser using a Raspberry Pi 4 Model B.

IMPORTANT POWER GUIDELINES
--------------------------
Do NOT draw power for the Servos, SIM800L module, or the Cooling Relay directly from the Raspberry Pi's 5V pins! These components draw significant current spikes that will cause the Raspberry Pi to brownout, crash, or reboot. 

You must use a dedicated, high-current 5V Power Supply (e.g., 5V 10A). 
Split the 5V and GND outputs from the power supply into a common power rail. Connect the power rail directly to:
1. The Raspberry Pi 4 (via GPIO 5V/GND or a USB-C breakout).
2. The SIM800L module VCC/GND.
3. The PCA9685 Servo Controller terminal block VCC/GND.
4. The Cooling Relay VCC/GND.

Ensure all components share a COMMON GROUND.

RASPBERRY PI GPIO PINOUT
------------------------

1. MEDICINE TAKEN BUTTON
   - Function: Physical button to dispense pills and silence alarms.
   - GPIO Pin: GPIO 17 (Pin 11)
   - Connection: Connect one leg of the button to GPIO 17, and the other leg to GND (Pin 9 or 14).

2. DHT11 TEMPERATURE SENSOR
   - Function: Monitors internal temperature for climate control.
   - GPIO Pin: GPIO 4 (Pin 7)
   - VCC: 3.3V (Pin 1) or 5V (Pin 2) depending on your DHT module.
   - GND: GND (Pin 6)
   - Data: Connect to GPIO 4. (If using a bare sensor, add a 4.7k-10k pull-up resistor between VCC and Data).

3. COOLING RELAY (FAN / PELTIER)
   - Function: Activates cooling when temp > 25°C.
   - GPIO Pin: GPIO 27 (Pin 13)
   - VCC: 5V (From Power Rail)
   - GND: GND (From Power Rail)
   - IN (Signal): Connect to GPIO 27.

4. PCA9685 SERVO CONTROLLER (I2C)
   - Function: Controls the 10 compartment servos.
   - SDA: GPIO 2 (Pin 3)
   - SCL: GPIO 3 (Pin 5)
   - VCC (Logic): 3.3V (Pin 1)
   - V+ (Motor Power): 5V (From Power Rail directly into the green terminal block!)
   - GND: GND (Pin 9)
   - Notes: Connect the 10 servos to Channels 0 through 9 on the PCA9685.

5. SIM800L GSM MODULE (UART)
   - Function: Sends SMS alerts and receives SMS commands.
   - TXD: GPIO 15 / RXD (Pin 10)  -> (Connect Pi RXD to SIM TXD)
   - RXD: GPIO 14 / TXD (Pin 8)   -> (Connect Pi TXD to SIM RXD)
   - VCC: 5V (From Power Rail) *Note: Some SIM800L modules require 3.7V - 4.2V. If yours specifically requires this, you must use a buck converter. If it's on a breakout board (like the red EVB boards), it usually accepts 5V.*
   - GND: GND (From Power Rail)

6. AUDIO
   - Function: Plays TTS alerts and sounds.
   - Audio Jack: Connect a standard 3.5mm speaker or amplifier to the Raspberry Pi's built-in audio jack. Alternatively, use a USB sound card. 

7. 3.5-INCH TFT DISPLAY (SPI) OVERLAP WARNING
   - The 3.5-inch 480x320 display is designed as a "HAT" that plugs directly into Pins 1-26 of the Raspberry Pi.
   - PROBLEM: If you plug the screen directly into the Pi, it physically blocks access to the I2C (Pins 3/5), UART (Pins 8/10), and GPIO pins you need for the other components!
   - SOLUTION 1 (Recommended): Use a "GPIO Expansion Board" or "Stackable Header" or "T-Cobbler". This allows you to plug the screen in on top, but gives you duplicate pins on the side to connect the SIM800L, PCA9685, DHT11, and Button.
   - SOLUTION 2 (Manual Wiring): Do not plug the screen directly onto the Pi. Instead, use jumper wires to connect only the required screen pins:
      * 5V (Pin 2) -> Screen VCC
      * GND (Pin 6) -> Screen GND
      * GPIO 11 (Pin 23) -> Screen SCLK (SPI Clock)
      * GPIO 9 (Pin 21) -> Screen MISO
      * GPIO 10 (Pin 19) -> Screen MOSI
      * GPIO 8 (Pin 24) -> Screen CE0 (Chip Select)
      * GPIO 25 (Pin 22) -> Screen Reset
      * GPIO 24 (Pin 18) -> Screen RS/DC
   - Note: Since you opted to use a physical button (GPIO 17) for dispensing instead of the broken touch controller on the Trixie OS, you do not need to wire the SPI Touch interrupt pins.
